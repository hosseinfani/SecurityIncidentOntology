2013-10-22

Robbery

Incident Details:

On Tuesday 22 October, 2013 Ryerson University Security and Emergency Services were made aware of the following incident:

 

At approximately 1 am, Security and Emergency Services came across a male non-community member near 40 Gerrard Street who reported that he had been robbed. 

 

The victim advised the two subjects had followed him from Yonge Street while he walked East bound on the Gerrard Street north sidewalk.  Subject 2 demanded he hand over his belongings.  The subjects then used what the victim believes to be a stun gun on his neck and stunned him repeatedly.  He fell down, and the subjects kicked him and stole several of his belongings.  Subject 1 then ran east on Gerrard Street and south on Church Street.  Subject 2 ran north through the laneway between the Ryerson Theatre School and 40 Gerrard Street.

 

The victim did not want medical attention and did not want to file a police report.  Security and Emergency Services made Toronto Police Services aware of the incident.

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.  

 

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).
