2014-11-11

Suspects Arrested Following Robbery

Incident Details:

On Sunday November 09, 2014, at approximately 12:55 AM, Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services were made aware of the following incident:

 

A few minutes earlier, the victim, a non-Ryerson community member, was approached by three subjects on the north sidewalk of Gould Street, next to the Library & Archives (LIB) building, at the intersection of Gould Street and Victoria Street.  The subjects demanded money from the victim and told him they had a firearm.  The victim gave his backpack to the subjects and they took other items, such as drinks and condiments from the subject’s cart before fleeing the area.

 

The victim was treated by Toronto Emergency Medical Services for minor injuries and declined any further medical assistance.  Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services attended the area, called Toronto Police Services, and located three suspects.  The suspects were arrested shortly thereafter, following the response by Ryerson's University Security & Emergency Services and Toronto Police Services.  The suspects are not believed to be Ryerson community members.  Toronto Police are continuing their investigation.

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca. For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Walk Safe escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at http://www.ryerson.ca/irm/security/index.html.
