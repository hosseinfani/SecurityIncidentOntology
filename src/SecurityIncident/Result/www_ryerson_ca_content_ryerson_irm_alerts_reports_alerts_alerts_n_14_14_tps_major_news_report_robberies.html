2014-04-21

Toronto Police Major News Report – Robberies

Incident Details:

Ryerson Security & Emergency Services would like to inform the Ryerson University community of a couple of robberies reported by Toronto Police Services. Below are the incidents listed on the Toronto Police Services “Major News Report” issued on 16 April, 2014. The first incident happened off Campus, although was believed by the Police to be related to the second incident, which occurred earlier, around Campus. Both incidents are believed to have occurred on April 16, 2014. 

 

“Robbery (Mugging) - 51 Division

 

  1. A 20 year old male reports that on May 16, 2014 at approximately 0300 hours, he was in the area of Church Street and Maitland Street when he was approached from behind by an unknown suspect. The suspect grabbed the victim around the neck and choked him until he lost consciousness. The suspect removed a cellular phone charger, personal identification and cash from the victim, and fled the scene in an unknown direction.”

 

 

“Robbery (Mugging) - 51 Division

 

  1. A 28 year old male reports that on April 16, 2013 at approximately 0015 hours, he was in the area of Gerrard Street East and Church Street when he was approached from behind by an unknown suspect. The suspect grabbed the victim around the neck, dragged him backwards and choked him until he lost consciousness. The suspect removed the victim’s cellular phone, iPod, eReader, headphones and identification documents and fled the scene in an unknown direction. The victim sustained injuries and was taken to the hospital by Toronto EMS.”

 

 

Both incidents can be found on the Toronto Police Major News reports page (http://www.torontopolice.on.ca/majors/?106) 

 

 

 If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.
