2014-03-19

Assault & Suspected Weapon Incident

Incident Details:

On Monday 17 March, 2014 at approximately 11:30 PM, community members reported an altercation in Victoria Laneway to Security & Emergency Services.

 

Security & Emergency Services attended the 350 Victoria Street area and observed the subject, who was topless, yelling at two males and a female walking southbound towards Gould Street. One of the male parties approached security and reported that the subject was yelling and screaming at passersby’s and had also punched him in the face. The victim did not want Police involvement and did not require medical assistance. The victim together with the female party left the area, informing Security & Emergency Services they were not community members.

 

The subject, still yelling at people, was approached by Security & Emergency Services and offered assistance locating his clothes. Open wounds which appeared to have been caused by an edged weapon were evident on the subject. The subject declined medical assistance and claimed not to remember when he had been injured or by whom. Toronto Police and Emergency Medical Services attended the area and commenced their investigation. The subject was taken to hospital by the paramedics.  It is not known where the subject received these injuries, or what they were caused by.

 

On 18 Tuesday, 2014 at approximately 01:30 AM, Security & Emergency Services located one of the two males whom the subject had been yelling at inside the Library Building. The second victim, who was a Ryerson student, reported being approached by the subject earlier while he had been walking southbound on Victoria Laneway towards Gould Street. The subject had used racial slurs towards the victim and had then assaulted him, injuring his foot. He declined medical attention. Toronto Police Services took a report from the second victim.   

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca. For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.  

 

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).
