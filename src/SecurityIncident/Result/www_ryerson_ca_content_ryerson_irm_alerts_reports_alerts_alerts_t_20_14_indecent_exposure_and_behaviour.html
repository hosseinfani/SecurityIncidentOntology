2014-07-20

Indecent Exposure & Behaviour

Incident Details:

On Thursday 17 July, 2014 Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services were made aware of the following incident:

At approximately 04:50 PM a community member was using the men’s washroom in the 350 Victoria Street main lobby. While the victim was washing his hands, he noticed the subject standing by the urinals staring at him. The victim finished washing his hands, turned around to exit, and noticed the subject masturbating with his genitals exposed while still staring at him. The victim noticed custodial services by the washroom entrance and motioned for them to enter. Shortly after, the subject covered himself and exited the washroom. The victim monitored the subject and contacted Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services.

The subject was last seen riding a bicycle heading west on Gould Street and then south on Yonge Street. The victim indicated during the incident the subject did not say anything towards him. The victim did not require Toronto Police Services involvement.

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca. For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Walk Safe escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at http://www.ryerson.ca/irm/security/index.html.

Support for students impacted by sexual violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).
