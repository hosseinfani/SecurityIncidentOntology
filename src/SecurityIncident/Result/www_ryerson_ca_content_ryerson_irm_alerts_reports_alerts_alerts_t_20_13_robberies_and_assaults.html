2013-08-27

Robberies and Assaults

Incident Details:

We would like to inform the Ryerson University community of a number of robberies and assaults reported by Toronto Police Services for the 23 to 25 August, 2013 weekend. Toronto Police Services reported a series of incidents involving robberies and assaults that occurred in the 51, 52 and 53 divisions. Below are 3 of the incidents, which occurred around the Ryerson University boundaries and were posted on Toronto Police Services “Major News Report” on 26 and 27 August, 2013.

 

 

  1. “A 43 year old male reports that on August 25, 2013 at approximately 0020 hours he was in the area of Yonge Street and Gerrard Street, when he was approached by a group of male suspects and one female suspect.  One suspect removed the victim’s cellular phone, while another suspect struck the victim in the face.  The suspects then fled the scene in an unknown direction.  The victim did not sustain any injuries.”
  2. “An 18 year old male reports that on August 25, 2013 at approximately 0035 hours, he was in the area of Dundas Street West and Yonge Street, when he was approached by two male suspects.  One of the suspects made a demand for the victim’s cellular phone and the second suspect struck the victim’s face with an unknown object.  The suspects then fled the scene in an unknown direction.  Toronto EMS attended and transported the victim to hospital for his injuries.”
  3. "A 20 year old male reports that on August 25, 2013 at approximately 0030 hours, he was in the area of Gould Street and Victoria Street, when he was approached by two male suspects.  One suspect made a demand for the victim’s property while the second suspect stood as a lookout.  The victim complied, and the second suspect kicked the victim to the ground.  The second suspect then punched the victim before fleeing the scene with suspect one in an unknown direction with the victim’s cellular phone.  Toronto EMS attended and transported the victim to the hospital for his injuries."

 

In addition to the above incidents, Ryerson Security & Emergency Services (SES) were made aware of the following incidents: An assault on Saturday 24 August, 2013, and an assault with a weapon on 27 August, 2013:

 

  1. At approximately 9:10 pm on August 24th, a male victim while on Victoria Laneway, between Gould Street and Gerrard Street, was approached by a male subject who was in a group of 4-5 males. The subject asked the victim what he was looking at, about three times, and then punched him on his nose. After the assault, the subject fled northbound on Victoria Laneway. Prior to the assault, a witness reported the same subject had initially approached him asking him what he was looking at. The subject then disengaged and walked away northbound, but soon returned and assaulted the victim. Toronto emergency services attended the area, and took over the investigation.  No detailed description is available.
  2. At approximately 1:30 am on August 27th, Security and Emergency Services (SES) received a report of an altercation at the Podium building upper balcony area. On arrival, involved parties had dispersed from the area. A male victim and 2 of his friends approached Security & Emergency Services shortly afterwards and advised he had been assaulted by Subject 1 with a pair of scissors resulting in a small puncture wound on the victim’s back.  The victim declined medical attention. Toronto Police Services attended and took over the investigation.  Subject 1 description: Female, medium complexion, 5’10”/1m 78 cm, athletic build, wearing black jeans and a black sleeveless shirt. Subject 2 description: Male, medium complexion, 5’6”/1m 68 cm, medium build, wearing a blue t-shirt and a sleeveless black shirt.

 

 

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.  

 

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).

 

Notification Information:

If you are a witness to a crime, please call Ryerson Security at 416-979-5040, or Toronto Police Services through Crime Stoppers at 416-222-TIPS (8477). 

For Emergencies dial "80" (internal phones only).
