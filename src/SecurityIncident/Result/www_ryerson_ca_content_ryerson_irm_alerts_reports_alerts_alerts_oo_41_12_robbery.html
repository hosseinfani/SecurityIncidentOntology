2012-10-02

Robbery

Incident Details:

On Monday 01 October, 2012 at approximately 9:25 pm the following incident was reported to Ryerson University Security and Emergency Services:

 

A male student was sitting at a table on Gould Street near Devo Pond.  He had placed his iPhone on the table.  Two men approached him, and while one distracted him through conversation, the other male stole his cell phone and ran westbound towards Yonge Street.  The first man then blocked the victim’s path, and left the area in the same direction once his associate had gotten away.

 

The victim was not physically injured. A search of the area was conducted but the men were not located.  A police report is being filed by the victim. 

 

If you have any information about these incidents or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.  

 

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counselling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).

 

Suspect information:

Subject 1 is described as:

Male, light complexion, 5’7” (1m 70 cm), thin build, 18-24 years of age, carrying a dark backpack.

 Subject 2 is described as:

Male, dark complexion, 5’7” (1m 70 cm), thin build, 18-24 years of age, carrying a football.
