2013-05-08

Suspicious Behaviour

Incident Details:

On Tuesday 07 May, 2013 Ryerson University Security and Emergency Services were made aware of the following incident:

 

At approximately 8:05 pm a female student was followed by a male subject from a store near campus in the area of Gould and Mutual streets. The subject followed her to her car which was parked nearby, introduced himself as “”Anthony”, and asked her several times if she could drive him where he wanted to go.  The student refused.  The subject then walked away and urinated against a campus building.  The subject returned, and tried to prevent the student from entering her car while swearing at her.  The student was able to enter her car.  The subject banged on her car window.  She drove away, and then notified Ryerson Security. The male then left walking southbound on Mutual Street. A search of the area was conducted but the man was not located.

 

The victim was not physically injured.  A report has been filed with Toronto Police Services.

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.  

 

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).
