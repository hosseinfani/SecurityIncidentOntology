2013-11-17

Suspicious Behaviour

Incident Details:

On Wednesday 13 November, 2013 Security & Emergency Services were made aware of the following incident:

 

At approximately 11:00 PM a female student was walking eastbound on the south side of Gould Street, crossing Church Street when she heard a vehicle honking at her. The student ignored the vehicle, although the vehicle followed her turning south on Dalhousie Street, which is a northbound one way Street. Subject 1, who was a passenger, yelled towards the student stating they “were looking for a girl” and asking for her phone number, her Facebook and offering to buy her a vehicle on her birthday. The student headed to her apartment and heard the vehicle’s door opening; the student hurried into the lobby. Subject 1 noticed people in the lobby, and quickly got back into the vehicle and drove off.

 

Security & Emergency Services checked the area for the vehicle which was described as an Orange Range Rover with a black top, but it was not located. The student was not physically injured in the incident. Toronto Police Services were notified of the incident.

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security & Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.  

 

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).
