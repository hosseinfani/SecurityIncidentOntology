2013-07-29

Robbery

Incident Details:

On 26 July, 2013 Toronto Police Services informed Ryerson University Security and Emergency Services of a robbery that occurred around the Yonge and Gould Street area on 24 July, 2013.The following was posted on the Toronto Police website:

 

 

“A 69 year old male reports that on July 24, 2013 at approximately 0210 hours he was in the area of Yonge Street and Gould Street when he was approached by a male suspect. The suspect left the scene and returned with another male suspect and a female suspect. The female suspect grabbed the victim’s wheelchair spinning it around. The victim was then struck in the head by one of the male suspects causing him to fall to the ground. The suspects removed a quantity of cash from the victim’s person. The suspects then fled the scene southbound on Yonge Street. No injuries were sustained by the victim. Police are requesting the assistance of the public in identifying the following described persons in connection with this offence. Description of Suspect #1:  Male, white, 5’6”, 141lbs to 150lbs, thin build, blond wavy hair. Suspect #2: Female, white, 20 to 25 years, heavy build, dark hair. Suspect #3: Male, white, 5’9”, thin build.”

 

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.  

 

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).
