2012-09-10

Sexual Assault

Incident Details:

On Saturday, 08 September, 2012 Toronto Police Services advised Ryerson Security and Emergency Services of a sexual assault that occurred at approximately 8:15 am:

 

A woman was walking westbound on Gould Street near Bond Street when a man walked up behind her, lifted her skirt, and grabbed her buttocks.  He then ran away northbound through the Quad. The woman was not physically injured.

 

The description of the man is similar to that of a man for whom Toronto Police issued an Alert for a similar sexual assault, which Security and Emergency Services shared with the Ryerson community on 05 September, 2012.

 

Toronto Police are continuing to investigate these incidents and are attempting to determine if the same person is responsible for multiple sexual assaults in the downtown area.

 
If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.   

 

Support for students impacted by sexual assault is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).

 

Suspect information:

Male

Dark complexion

Early 20’s

Medium Build

Approximately 6’ 2” or 6’3” / 1m 88 – 90 cm tall

Black hooded sweatshirt with white writing across the chest, a patch on one of the shoulders, dark grey sweat pants, black and white running shoes
