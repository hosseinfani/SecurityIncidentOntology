2012-10-30

Robbery

Incident Details:

On Tuesday 30 October, 2012 at approximately 1:15 pm a male student was walking Westbound between Pitman Hall and the Rogers Communications Centre while texting on his cell phone.  Two males approached him from behind.  Subject 1 asked him who he was texting, and pushed him into a wall. He demanded that the victim hand over his cell phone, which the victim did, and then asked him what else he had.  The victim handed over his wallet. Subject 1 then pushed the victim to the ground.  He and the second male then ran away eastbound to Mutual Street, west on Gould Street, and southbound on Dalhousie Street.  

The victim and a witness followed the suspects and attempted to get help from people in the area.  When they reached the area of Dalhousie and Dundas Street Subject 1 turned around and told them to stop following him and that he had a knife.  No weapon was seen.  The subjects then headed Westbound on Dundas Street. 

A number of witnesses contacted Security and Emergency Services.  Toronto Police attended and are investigating the incident.  Ambulance personnel treated the victim for minor physical injuries. 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.    

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).
