2014-11-11

Voyeurism

Incident Details:

On Saturday November 08, 2014, at approximately 5:30 PM, Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services were made aware of the following incident:

 

Approximately fifteen minutes earlier, the victim, a Ryerson community member, observed a mobile phone being held near the space between the washroom stall and wall, on the other side of the adjacent stall in the men’s washroom, located on the 8th floor of the Library & Archives (LIB) building.  The victim then observed a phone being held over the stall that he was occupying by the subject who had been occupying the adjacent stall.  The victim exited the stall and contacted Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services. 

 

As the subject exited the adjacent stall, the victim advised the subject that he had seen him taking pictures and asked to see the subject’s phone.  The subject denied taking any pictures, unlocked his phone, and appeared to be browsing and deleting pictures.  The subject then showed the victim his phone, which appeared to be a different type of phone from that which he was last seen holding, and said that there were not any pictures on his phone.  At this time, the victim and subject exited the washroom and the subject was last seen leaving in the direction of the stairs or elevators.

 

Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services conducted safety planning with the victim and a patrol of the area, but did not locate a person matching the subject’s description.  The victim reported no physical injuries, declined medical attention, and did not want Toronto Police Services involved at this time.

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Ryerson University Security & Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.
