2012-02-03

UPDATE - SYRINGE ON DOOR HANDLE

Incident Details:

On Thursday 02 February, 2012 at approximately 1:40 pm a male non-community
member was arrested by Toronto Police Services in connection with this
incident.

The Subject is described as:
Male
Medium complexion
5'11", thin build
Mid 40's
Short dark hair, black goatee
Wearing a maroon leather jacket, blue jeans, black winter boots
Overall neat appearance

Monday 30 January, 2012

Syringe Fastened to Door Handle
    

On Saturday 28 January, 2012 at approximately 6:35 pm a Ryerson Security and
Emergency Services Officer found a syringe had been fastened to the top
inside part of a door handle with plastic shrink wrap.  The needle's tip had
been bent at an angle.  The purpose of this appears to have been to have the
needle penetrate the hand of someone opening the door.   The door in
question was the card reader door at the 99 Gerrard Street entrance to the
EPH/SHE building.   The Security Officer attended hospital but was not
injured.

Ryerson Security and Emergency Services and Toronto Police Services are
investigating this incident.   

We ask that everyone be alert and extra cautious, especially in the area of
this occurrence.   If you notice anything similar do not touch the item but
call Security right away.

If you have any information about this incident or noticed anyone acting
suspiciously in the area around the time of the incident please contact
Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040.

Notification Information:

If you are a witness to a crime, please call Ryerson Security at 416-979-5040, or Toronto Police Services through Crime Stoppers at 416-222-TIPS (8477). 

For Emergencies dial "80" (internal phones only).
