2013-02-05

Attempted Assault

Incident Details:

On Monday 04 February, 2013 at approximately 12:30 am Ryerson University Security and Emergency Services observed a male subject chasing two female students with a hockey stick while attempting to hit them.  The students had been walking east bound on Gould Street when the subject tried to assault them.  The subject fled the area eastbound and then south bound on Jarvis Street upon seeing Security.

 

No one was physically injured.  Toronto Police Services and Ryerson Security and Emergency Services are continuing to investigate this incident.

 

If you have any information about this incident or have been the victim of a similar incident, please call Security and Emergency Services at 416-979-5040 or via email at security@ryerson.ca.  For any incidents in progress use the Emergency "80" number from internal phones.  Security & Emergency Services offers 24 hour Safe Walk escorts and free self-defense courses; please check our website for more information at www.ryerson.ca/security.  

 

Support for students impacted by violence is available through Student Development and Counseling (416-979-5195, http://www.ryerson.ca/counselling/).

Suspect information:

Male

Light Complexion

5’9” / 1m 75 cm tall

Approximately 50-60 years of age

White scruffy beard

Wearing a forest green winter jacket, black track pants, black toque

Overall unkempt appearance
